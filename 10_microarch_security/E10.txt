Exercise 1

Consistent pattern across 10 runs:

- array[3×4096] and array[7×4096] (pre-cached): consistently 20-40 cycles → Cache Hits

- Other elements (not pre-cached): consistently 200-1140 cycles → Cache Misses

    → clear gap between hits and misses, confirming the effectiveness of the cache attack in distinguishing between cached and non-cached data.

    Threshold of 100-120 cycles is reasonable to differentiate hits from misses, as hits are well below this threshold and misses are well above it.

gcc -march=native flush-reload.c -o flush-reload -O2 -lpthread

./flush-reload n times:

...

I didn't get 94 consistently but I did get 94 a few times, and I also got 3 and 7 consistently, which are the expected results for the attack. The presence of 94 in some runs could be due to noise or other factors affecting the timing measurements, but the consistent appearance of 3 and 7 indicates that the attack is successfully leaking information about the accessed indices.

The CPU is prefetching, so when you access array[94], it also loads neighboring cache lines/indices into the TLB/cache, causing false positives from elements like 95, 96, etc.
